    SDG 13
    Climate action

    One of the 17 goals established by the United Nations in the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. It focuses on taking urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. Climate change is a universal challenge that disrupts national economies, affects lives, and leads to significant economic losses. Every country is experiencing the effects, including changing weather patterns, rising sea levels, and increased frequency of extreme weather events.

    HUMAN HEALTH
    AND SAFETY

    The impacts of climate change pose serious health risks, including heat-related illnesses, respiratory issues from poor air quality, and increased spread of diseases. Vulnerable populations are particularly at risk, making it essential to address these challenges.

    FOOD SECURITY
    AND AGRICULTURE

    Climate change threatens agricultural production and food security. Extreme weather events can lead to crop failures, affecting food supply and prices, which in turn impacts nutrition and health.

    INFRASTRUCTURE
    AND SERVICES

    The increasing intensity of climate-related disasters damages critical infrastructure, disrupting essential services such as water supply, sanitation, education, energy, and transport. This can lead to long-term socio-economic challenges.

    GREENHOUSE GAS
    EMISSION

    Human activities are the primary drivers of climate change, with greenhouse gas emissions at their highest levels in history. SDG 13 emphasizes the need for urgent action to reduce these emissions and transition to sustainable practices.
